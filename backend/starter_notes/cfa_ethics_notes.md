# CFA Institute Code of Ethics & Standards of Professional Conduct

The CFA Institute Code of Ethics and Standards of Professional Conduct are fundamental to the integrity of the investment profession. The standards are divided into seven categories, which are highly tested on Level I, II, and III of the CFA exams.

---

## Standard I: Professionalism

### A. Knowledge of the Law
Members and candidates must understand and comply with all applicable laws, rules, and regulations of any government, regulatory organization, licensing agency, or professional association governing their professional activities.
* **Key Guidelines**: 
  - In the event of a conflict, members/candidates must comply with the more strict law, rule, or regulation.
  - Members must not knowingly participate or assist in, and must dissociate themselves from, any violation of laws, rules, or regulations.
  - Dissociation involves requesting the activity stop, or removing one's name from reports. In extreme cases, resignation from the firm is required. Reporting to authorities is optional/governed by local law, but dissociation is mandatory.

### B. Independence and Objectivity
Members and candidates must use reasonable care and judgment to achieve and maintain independence and objectivity in their professional activities.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - Do not offer, solicit, or accept any gift, benefit, compensation, or consideration that reasonably could be expected to compromise independence and objectivity.
  - **Travel/Lodging**: For business diligence trips sponsored by companies, the "pay-your-own-way" standard is the best practice. Members should pay for their own commercial transport and lodging. High-end, lavish accommodation or luxury travel sponsored by brokers is a violation.
  - **Research Reports**: Analysts must not write favorable reports in exchange for investment banking mandates, advisory roles, or direct monetary compensation.

### C. Misrepresentation
Members and candidates must not knowingly make any misrepresentations relating to investment analysis, recommendations, actions, or other professional activities.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - Do not guarantee investment returns on volatile, non-guaranteed instruments.
  - Plagiarism is strictly prohibited. You must cite sources when using third-party research, models, estimates, or charts, unless using generic macroeconomic statistical data from official agencies.

### D. Misconduct
Members and candidates must not engage in any professional conduct involving dishonesty, fraud, or deceit or commit any act that reflects adversely on their professional reputation, integrity, or competence.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - Covers behavior in professional and personal environments that reflects on financial integrity. 
  - Personal bankruptcy or minor traffic violations are generally not misconduct, but cheating on an exam, committing tax fraud, or public intoxication during work events violates this standard.

## Standard II: Integrity of Capital Markets

### A. Material Nonpublic Information
Members and candidates who possess material nonpublic information that could affect the value of an investment must not act or cause others to act on the information.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - **Material**: Information is material if its disclosure would likely have an impact on the price of a security, or if a reasonable investor would want to know it before making an investment decision.
  - **Nonpublic**: Information is nonpublic until it has been disseminated to the general marketplace (e.g., via a press release, public filing, or widely circulated news conference).
  - **Mosaic Theory**: Analysts can reach investment conclusions based on a combination of public information and nonmaterial nonpublic information. Using these pieces together to form a buy/sell rating does not violate this standard.

### B. Market Manipulation
Members and candidates must not engage in practices that distort prices or artificially inflate trading volume with the intent to mislead market participants.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - Covers transaction-based manipulation (e.g., executing trades to artificially drive up volume or price) and information-based manipulation (e.g., spreading false rumors on forums to pump a stock).

## Standard III: Duties to Clients

### A. Loyalty, Prudence, and Care
Members and candidates have a duty of loyalty to their clients and must act with reasonable care and exercise prudent judgment.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - Place client interests before employer or personal interests at all times.
  - Votes proxies in an informed and responsible manner, keeping in mind the long-term economic value to the client.

### B. Fair Dealing
Members and candidates must deal fairly and objectively with all clients when providing investment analysis, making investment recommendations, or taking investment action.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - "Fairly" does not mean "equally". You can offer premium services (like a dedicated advisory line) for higher fees, but all clients must be notified of research changes at the same time.
  - Allocate trades fairly (pro-rata based on order size) for all client accounts. Do not favor personal accounts or specific favored clients.

### C. Suitability
When members and candidates are in an advisory relationship with a client, they must:
1. Make a reasonable inquiry into a client's investment experience, risk and return objectives, and financial constraints prior to making any investment recommendation or taking investment action.
2. Determine that an investment is suitable to the client's financial situation and consistent with the client's written objectives (Investment Policy Statement - IPS) before recommending or taking action.
3. Evaluate suitability in the context of the client's total portfolio, not in isolation.

### D. Performance Presentation
When communicating investment performance, members and candidates must make reasonable efforts to ensure that it is fair, accurate, and complete.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - Do not state or imply that past performance guarantees future results.
  - Adhering to the Global Investment Performance Standards (GIPS) is highly recommended.

### E. Preservation of Confidentiality
Members and candidates must keep information about current, former, and prospective clients confidential unless:
1. The information concerns illegal activities on the part of the client.
2. Disclosure is required by law.
3. The client or prospective client permits disclosure of the information.

## Standard IV: Duties to Employers

### A. Loyalty
In matters related to their employment, members and candidates must act for the benefit of their employer and not deprive their employer of the advantage of their skills and abilities, divulge confidential information, or otherwise cause harm to their employer.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - **Independent Practice**: You must obtain written consent from both your employer and the external clients before undertaking independent practice that competes with your employer.
  - **Leaving a Firm**: When leaving, you must not take client lists, proprietary models, or records without express permission. You may use skills and general knowledge acquired, but not physical/electronic data sheets.

### B. Additional Compensation Arrangements
Members and candidates must not accept gifts, benefits, compensation, or consideration that competes with or might reasonably be expected to create a conflict of interest with their employer's interest unless they obtain written consent from all parties involved.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - This covers bonuses paid by clients based on future portfolio performance. Written consent must be obtained *before* accepting the agreement.

### C. Responsibilities of Supervisors
Members and candidates must make reasonable efforts to ensure that anyone subject to their supervision or authority complies with applicable laws, rules, regulations, and the Code and Standards.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - You cannot delegate supervisory responsibility. If a supervisor detects a violation or a weak compliance system, they must take action, implement procedures, and limit the violator's activities until an investigation concludes.

## Standard V: Investment Analysis, Recommendations, and Actions

### A. Diligence and Reasonable Basis
Members and candidates must:
1. Exercise diligence, independence, and thoroughness in analyzing investments, making recommendations, and taking investment actions.
2. Have a reasonable and adequate basis, supported by appropriate research and investigation, for any investment analysis, recommendation, or action.

### B. Communication with Clients and Prospective Clients
Members and candidates must:
1. Disclose to clients and prospective clients the basic format and general principles of the investment processes they use to analyze investments, select securities, and construct portfolios and must promptly disclose any changes that materially affect those processes.
2. Disclose significant limitations and risks associated with the investment process.
3. Use reasonable judgment in identifying which factors are important to their investment analyses, recommendations, or actions and include those factors in communications with clients and prospective clients.
4. Distinguish between fact and opinion in the presentation of investment analyses and recommendations.

### C. Record Retention
Members and candidates must develop and maintain appropriate records to support their investment analyses, recommendations, actions, and other investment-related communications with clients and prospective clients.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - In the absence of regulatory guidance, the CFA Institute recommends retaining all research and record files for at least **7 years**.

## Standard VI: Conflicts of Interest

### A. Disclosure of Conflicts
Members and candidates must make full and fair disclosure of all matters that could reasonably be expected to impair their independence and objectivity or interfere with respective duties to their clients, prospective clients, and employer.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - Common conflicts include stock ownership in recommended companies, board directorships, referral fees, and family connections.

### B. Priority of Transactions
Investment transactions for clients and employers must have priority over investment transactions in which a member or candidate is the beneficial owner.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - Client accounts must be filled first.
  - Employer accounts are filled second.
  - Personal accounts (beneficial owner) must be traded *after* clients and employers have had adequate opportunity to act on a recommendation.

### C. Referral Fees
Members and candidates must disclose to their employer, clients, and prospective clients, as appropriate, any compensation, consideration, or benefit received from or paid to others for the recommendation of products or services.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - Disclosure allows clients to evaluate any potential biases in the referral recommendation.

---

## Standard VII: Responsibilities as a CFA Institute Member or CFA Candidate

### A. Conduct as Participants in CFA Institute Programs
Members and candidates must not engage in any conduct that compromises the reputation or integrity of CFA Institute or the CFA designation or the integrity, security, or administration of CFA Institute programs.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - Do not share specific, detailed questions from the exam.
  - Do not cheat, use unauthorized calculators, or disrupt testing facilities.

### B. Reference to CFA Institute, the CFA Designation, and the CFA Program
When referring to CFA Institute, the CFA designation, or the CFA program, members and candidates must not misrepresent or exaggerate the meaning or implications of membership in CFA Institute, holding the CFA designation, or candidacy in the CFA program.
* **Key Guidelines**:
  - "CFA" must always be used as an adjective, never a noun (e.g., "Aryan is a CFA charterholder" or "Aryan is a CFA candidate" is correct. "Aryan is a CFA" is incorrect).
  - Do not claim that the CFA charter guarantees superior investment performance or implies superior analytical talent.
